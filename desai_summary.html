"There's a lot of hoo-hah about the "perceptual-motor grounding" of language. We looked at the processing of metaphors like "grasp an idea" and found a very interesting and satisfying pattern of results. Such metaphors do activate corresponding sensory/motor brain systems but only if they are relatively unfamiliar. More familiar metaphoric expressions and abstract expressions ("understand the idea") do not. People's comprehension is not limited to what can be physically embodied, folks."
